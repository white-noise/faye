“I don’t think this way leads to a road,” I say, “We must be between two buildings still.”
“It takes us to where we need to go.”
“And where is that?” I ask.
“Just a ways further. The journey is not a long one. People think it is, but it’s not. Why don’t you play your tape, while we are walking. The one from before.”
“I would prefer not to,” I say.
“While this journey is not long, child, it feels even shorter if you listen to what you came here with. This place is not so strange, or so beyond belief, but it is certainly a place which, once entered, must be left. And things which are brought in should be used. It is like how you might want a story to be. Everything has a place.”
I can either play the tape, or ask the pumpkin instead for a story of its own.
