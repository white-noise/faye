I’m trying to compose an email to you. And usually this big electronic machine makes me excited, and it feels like I can compose anything. But tonight it feels heavy like a rock. I can talk to anyone in the world, and I’ve got nothing to say.
You always said that when you were caught up in something you liked to take a walk. They cleared your head, and helped you focus on what was right in front of you, you said. It sounds so simple; how could it be right?
Do you think that we’re distracted, sometimes, by things coming to us immediately, or leaving us at a lightning pace? Like with this big electronic thing? In some ways it feels like it doesn’t matter, but in another sense I want to know if it makes things different. It certainly feels like it could be.
Anyway, none of this is good for an email. Gotta abandon the whole thing.
So I go for a walk.
