The roads are perfectly painted. I’ve always liked this, even if it seems like the paint gets neater when times get worse. As if everyone drops what they are doing and goes to work on them.
Although of course that’s not true. People keep on doing what they’ve been doing.
I cross over crisply painted crosswalks and past highly reflective road signs that, at this time of night, glow murkily. It’s not as bright here as in the major cities. I can still see some stars, and the moon. I can see the glow of some larger city to the east.
It is the first day of November, and the trees are starting to drop their leaves.
Everything seems a little like it is dying.
