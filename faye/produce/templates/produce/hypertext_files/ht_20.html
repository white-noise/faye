Standing here makes me feel like a child. So does wearing this big coat.
I remember the Walkman and take it out. It is worn around its edges, which flare a dull silver. It used to be that everyone had one of these, but in the past couple of years people have moved onto CDs, or flash memory. Everything is getting smaller; I have no idea what to make of that. People cling so closely to their favorite things, and then before you know it, they’re disappearing in your hands—I don’t even feel that old.
There’s a tape in the Walkman. I press the power button. A light.
I press play.
