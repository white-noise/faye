No sound, of course.
I need headphones. I check in the other pocket of the jacket. Nothing. I check in the breast-pocket.
A small pair of headphones, knotted with itself.
I untie them, plug them in, and press play.
At first only static. Then a scraping noise, like chairs being moved across a floor. I hear my dad’s clipped low voice, and then the tape breaks into singing “Happy Birthday.” It’s being sung to me.
There are at least three voices, the sounds of shuffling feet, straining high notes. The song’s final croon folds up into silence and static, and I hear someone, the young me, blowing out candles. I am asked how old I am. It’s my dad asking. He tries to ask me about my wish, but I don’t tell him. I am being taunted. He starts to ask again but the sound cuts. Quiet. I press stop.
back to home
