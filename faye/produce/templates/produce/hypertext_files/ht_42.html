The walk is almost too short back to the shrine. But distance is not how I thought it was. The world is connected by invisible shortnesses. I wish I could say that I dwelled there, in the shrine, and looked for signs of what had come to pass, or read scripture on the walls, or signs in the stars, but I just continued through.
I had stood by the stone lantern long enough, in the past. And in a way, this time was no different.
I do check the small stone house. But it is empty.
