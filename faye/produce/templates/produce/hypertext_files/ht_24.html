Inside of the small house is a kabocha pumpkin, carved circular eyes aflame. I peer into them, keeping an ear out for the voice I heard before. The kabocha’s mouth is a wide grin, carved roughly with a knife. Jack ‘O’ Lanterns aren’t uncommon, but they’re certainly not Shinto or Buddhist. I start to poke around for a speaker or microphone.
“I’m trying to die.” The words come from in front of me. I let the lighter lapse again. The kabocha’s eyes take up the slack, throwing orange streaks on fine grass growing between the bricks.
“Is that you?” I ask.
“Of course,” the kabocha replies. Its voice is calm, low.
“And you want to die?”
“I have tried for a long time, and I don’t want any chance of blowing it, so to speak.”
“What do you mean?” I ask.
“Lean in, and look at my eyes, child.”
