The waxy leaves are blue black in the light. All the vines are twisted around each other. I wonder if someone could train vines to be like this, or if it took some sort of careful disinterest. I can’t imagine that this formed while anyone was looking. It’s too wild. Maybe my looking has stopped it in its tracks.
I hear a sound.
It’s coming from under the hedge. I wait.
No more sound comes.
I approach the entryway to the alley, and crouch down. I don’t think it is being brave so much as trying to understand whatever fears I can. There are plenty of fears I can’t know, no matter how much I lean in toward them.
A black cat steps out from the bush, looking at me.
I startle backwards, and something clatters from my jacket pocket onto the street. The cat, frightened at the noise, slips into a bullet of darkness down the street. It tangles itself with the shadows and disappears.
I reach for what fell.
It’s a tape player, Walkman brand. What you used to sell.
I continue into the alley.
