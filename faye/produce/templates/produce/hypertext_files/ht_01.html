A child lives in a small city near a harbor—a place built to make steel and still existing because there was no great need, no great haste to destroy it. And besides, there was enough to do, to watch, to be absorbed in. And so the city changed in every way. Often the child is awake late at night, when small cities feel most empty. But, if pressed, it would be hard for them to say whether or not they felt alone.
 Such things are not certain, these days.
