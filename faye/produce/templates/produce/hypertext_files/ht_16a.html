Away from the vending machine, everything is darker again. I feel chilled inside. I can taste sugar on my lips, the buzz of carbonation, and though it is getting close to winter, the taste reminds me of summer. City parks blazing with sunlight.
I see the alley with hedges surrounding it, similar to how I remember from so many times. It seems smaller, maybe, since the last time I saw it, but no less wild.
I am not sure what happens to things in my memory when they are not accessed for a long time. But I know that everyone has things they don’t think about. So whatever happens, I am not alone in it.
I clutch the tape recorder. It has a metal chassis: one of the old ones from the late 70s, almost before I was born.
I hear a noise under the hedge, but push down the alley and don’t think about it. The world isn’t about figuring out every noise or movement; think about them all and you’ll lose it.
