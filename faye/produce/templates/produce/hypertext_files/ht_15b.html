I want to keep going. I’ve waited around too long, and if I keep standing here, then this will be just like the email. It is like when a machine grinds to a halt because it hasn’t been kept in use. Where no matter how much you oil or clean it, what really keeps it all going is constant motion and some concomitant will not to fail. I don’t know how people fix a machine like that.
You always said you weren’t very good at fixing things. Just that you were good at telling other people that they could fix things: that it was possible for them. But that’s most of the game, isn’t it?
In order to fix something, you have to believe you can fix it; there is no other way.
I guess, in a way, I am still figuring out if this is what I believe.
One of the alleyways is framed by a large, overgrown hedge. It’s the one I’ve been walking toward.
I go toward the hedge.
