I walk off toward the trees. They come faster than expected, and soon my feet are surrounded by tough grass, firmer and more resisting than what filled the cemetery. Nevertheless, I feel more grounded. Less confused. Grass becomes shrubs, which become trees, and soon the canopy only permits brief glimpses of starlight. The candles in my hand seem to grow in brightness.
The path is easy to lose, though every time I worry that I have strayed off it, the ground ahead seems to reveal some secret sign, in the light, that assures me I am tending toward somewhere.
Then I come upon a grove of trees.
And you are in the tree.
