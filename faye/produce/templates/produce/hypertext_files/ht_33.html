“Why did you stop the tape?” The kabocha asks.
I tell it that the tape is broken up into sections by pauses. And there is no guarantee that I should be listening to any of them. It is safer to stop when prompted.
The kabocha says it is wise to stop at absences, true, which are themselves an important part of the whole collection of presences around them. But sooner or later, the kabocha says, a pause has to end. Otherwise, it says, how can you be sure it was a pause at all?
I can ask the pumpkin for a story this time, or play the tape recorder again.
