“What will happen to you, when I leave you here? In truth?”
“I will forget who I am,” says the kabocha, “I will do whatever things do when they die. I will make a difference, if only in having had a beginning and an end. In being between two gulfs of non-existence.”
“Is that enough?” I ask, “Against the pain it causes others?”
“Absence informs presence, if that makes sense, child, but it is also the root of all cruelty. I do not know the answer to your question. Just that I am here, and sometime later, I will be not.”
