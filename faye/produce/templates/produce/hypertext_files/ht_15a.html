I’m not ready to keep going where I’ve been leading myself, as funny as that sounds. I see a vending machine, down the alley, and decide to walk to it. Soda still calms me, even though you don’t buy it for me anymore.
It is the only truly lighted thing around. The sodium yellow streetlamps barely illuminate themselves. The light of the vending machine is cold blue and radiant against the night. Plastic models of all the drinks for sale are arrayed behind clear plastic.
I choose lemon soda. November isn’t the time to be drinking lemon soda, but I don’t care.
I drink half, and then put the bottle in the pocket of my large coat.
I feel the bottle hit something. I take it out.
It’s a tape player, Walkman brand. I place the bottle back into my coat pocket, grasping the new object, and walk back to the street.
back to home
