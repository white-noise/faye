I am at a loss. And even though I am in a forest I feel surrounded by nothing alive. The kabocha feels incredibly far away.
And then the cat, the one I saw so long ago, appears, weaving between my legs. I look away from you to see its sleek black fur tracing a loose figure eight in the darkness. It nuzzles my leg, and I bend down to it. Scratching its head.
The cat spits a smooth white seed into my hand.
A pumpkin seed.
I run my hand down the length of its back, and then it is gone. Perhaps to follow some other child somewhere. I do not know much of such things.
I offer the seed to you, and you accept it. You begin to talk about our garden, but I know it is both you and not you. I also know that, when you are lost in memory, it means I have given you what was required. You pine for days passed.
I ask politely to leave.
And like all ghosts, you keep your promises.
