I open my unlocked door and stand in the entryway. Cold air creeps along my back until I close the door behind me, and then it is quieter. No sounds of wind, no sounds of rustling leaves or creaking branches. For all people say about haunted houses, this one was remarkably dead. Nothing stirred. I was like a furnishing in it.
I ease up the stairs, but they are not especially tricky now, almost welcoming, and my feet find the long carpet of the computer room again. I sink into it a little. I will have no answer when my dad comes asking for his coat.
I sit down in the chair in front of the computer.
And find my blank email still waiting for me.
