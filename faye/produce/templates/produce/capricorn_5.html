December 23, 2023.

It’s too warm this morning, even before
The sun makes low transit across the Midwest sky.
Such mellow catastrophe, laid across my forehead
Like a kiss, or a poultice meant for the very ill.

It upsets something in the jelly of my eyes,
Slowing the solidification of sleep’s fluids,
Leaving me time to think, stunned by blindness,
Of your spoon-shaped ear and morning voice.

Something devastating in the barometry of it all,
In the beautiful warm winter, sick as a dog.
Night comes so early that sleep is now one room in
A very large house. I am so embarrassed to wake you.

But I do, and you stretch; you drink your cool water.
You open the window so the cat might smell the air.
